Best Movies/Series According to Adegboyega

Here are my top 3 favoirite movies/series of January 2025.

Moana 2

After the adventures of the first movie, Moana has become the chief of Motunui. She continues to explore the ocean with her people and discovers an ancient map leading to a lost island said to hold a powerful and mysterious force. With her trusty friend Maui, the demigod, and new allies along the way, she sets sail on a journey filled with dangers and magical creatures to find the island and uncover its secrets. This quest tests her leadership, bravery, and determination as she encounters challenges that threaten her homeland and her people's way of life.

Kingsman: The Secret Service

"Kingsman: The Secret Service" follows the story of a young man named Eggsy, whose life takes a dramatic turn when he is recruited by a secret spy organization known as Kingsman. Eggsy, a street-smart teenager with untapped potential, is introduced to the world of elite espionage by Harry Hart, a veteran Kingsman agent. As Eggsy undergoes rigorous training and tests, he learns the values of courage, loyalty, and sophistication. The Kingsman organization faces a formidable threat from Richmond Valentine, a tech mogul with a diabolical plan to solve the world's overpopulation problem through mass genocide. With stylish action sequences, witty dialogues, and a touch of British elegance, "Kingsman: The Secret Service" delivers an exciting and unconventional spy thriller that redefines the genre.

His House

"His House" follows the harrowing journey of a refugee couple, Bol and Rial, who flee war-torn South Sudan and seek asylum in England. Upon being granted temporary residency, they are placed in a dilapidated house in a small town. As they try to adjust to their new life, they soon discover that their new home is haunted by a sinister presence. The couple is tormented by nightmarish visions and ghostly apparitions, forcing them to confront their past traumas and the dark secrets that followed them. The film expertly blends psychological horror with social commentary, exploring themes of displacement, guilt, and cultural identity. With its chilling atmosphere and powerful performances, "His House" delivers a thought-provoking and deeply unsettling experience that lingers long after the credits roll.
